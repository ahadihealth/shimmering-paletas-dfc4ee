Our Story

One promise, kept two ways

"Ahadi" is Swahili for promise. It started as our promise to residents in long-term care who needed wound healing brought to them. It now extends to a promise for patients and families who want a primary care relationship built on trust, not turnover.

Compassionate

Every patient's journey is treated as its own — not a protocol to move through.

Evidence-Based

Clinical decisions grounded in current wound care and primary care best practice.

Faith-Aligned

Our values guide how we show up for patients, families, and facility partners alike.

Mobile Wound Care serves facilities across Kansas. Direct Primary Care is enrolling members in Kansas and Missouri.

Privacy & Security

How we handle your information

We only ask for what we need to arrange your care, and every form on this site requires your consent before submitting. Facility referrals intentionally exclude resident names and birthdates until a referral is confirmed.

Note for the site owner: this page is a static front-end demo. Collecting health information (PHI) in a way that's actually HIPAA-compliant is an infrastructure and process commitment, not just page copy — it requires a secure backend, encryption in transit and at rest, access controls and audit logs, signed Business Associate Agreements with any hosting or form vendor, staff training, and a real Notice of Privacy Practices reviewed by counsel. None of that exists in this mockup yet; treat every "submitted" form here as a design preview only.
